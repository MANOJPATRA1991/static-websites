If d(x,y)d(x,y)d(x,y) is the distance between two points xxx and yyy, then following properties hold:

  1. Positivity

  2. Symmetry
    a) d(x,y)=d(y,x)d(x,y) = d(y,x)d(x,y)=d(y,x) for all x and y

  3. Triangle inequality
    a) d(x,z)≤d(x,y)+d(y,z)d(x,z) \leq d(x,y) + d(y,z)d(x,z)≤d(x,y)+d(y,z) for all points x, y and z

The measures that satisfy all three properties are called metrics
